An Intelligent PDF Reader

Gokul

Tablets and mobiles are convenient, eco-friendly document readers that can carry millions of books. Oftentimes, the reader may have to recite prayers, or read out a bedtime story or be engaged in other activities like cooking when one’s hands are dirty and one would like to avoid touching the phone or tablet. In such use cases, it is useful to have a voice controlled PDF app, and even better is an intelligent PDF app. Under the voice control feature, the PDF reader responds to commands like “go to page 10” via the microphone and does the respective action on the PDF viewer. On the other hand, the intelligence feature is intended for readers who would recite the contents of the PDF aloud. For such users, it is very inconvenient to simultaneously focus on fixing the right zoom, and location using their hands instead of focusing on their recitation performance. An intelligent PDF reader is envisioned to automatically scroll to the right place and provide the right level of zoom that is comfortable to the eye. In this project we propose to address these needs. These features need (1) Voice to text conversion, (2) PDF to text conversion, and (3) a metric assess how close (1) and (2) match to access the location of the PDF and zoom accordingly. However, Voice control needs only (1). As a part of this course we successfully completed the first phase on audio recognition, and partly implemented text recognition corresponding to second phase. We built an app deployed it on our mobiles and tablets, and is quite accurate; 90% of times it recognizes and does the correct action based on the command.
